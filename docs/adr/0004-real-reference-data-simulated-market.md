# ADR-0004: Ground a simulated market in real reference data

Status: accepted

## Context

"Realism" could mean either reproducing the live airline market or building a
plausible simulation from real-world constraints. A live replica would require
continually updated schedules, fares, fleets, and competitors, with substantial
licensing and operational complexity.

## Decision

The game will use real-world reference data for aircraft types, airports,
geography, and relevant operational constraints. Passenger demand, fares,
costs, fuel markets, and competitive conditions will be generated by the game
and designed to produce plausible, explainable outcomes.

Players operate fictional airlines. The game does not claim to mirror live
airlines or current commercial schedules.

## Consequences

- Reference-data provenance and licensing must be tracked.
- Simulation formulas and generated inputs must be internally consistent and
  explainable to players.
- The game can evolve independently of daily changes in the real airline
  market.
- Real-world facts and simulated values must remain visibly distinct in the
  data model and administrative tooling.
