# ADR-0025: Fund new careers with equity and optional credit

Status: accepted

## Context

An airline that receives only an aircraft lease may be unable to fund staff,
fuel, maintenance, and early route losses. Excessive free cash would make route
economics and financing irrelevant. Debt should support calculated expansion
without becoming an arbitrary level reward.

## Decision

A new career airline receives standardized founder equity sized to provide a
reasonable operating runway for its starter lease. It may also accept a modest
optional founding loan.

Later loan availability and pricing derive from creditworthiness, including
collateral, profitability, leverage, safety standing, and repayment history.
Cash grants are not awarded for reaching abstract levels.

## Consequences

- Starting balance must be tested against every founder aircraft and a viable
  starter network.
- The onboarding forecast should show expected runway before the player commits
  to a starter package.
- Debt requires repayment schedules, interest, default, collateral, and lender
  remedies.
- Creditworthiness remains an explainable domain calculation, not an XP gate.
